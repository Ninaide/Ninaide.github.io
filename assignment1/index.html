A Review of Downstream

My initial reaction when interacting with the Downstream interface was the density of options on the page and the utilisation of webpage space.

Over the span of 2 minutes, my focus when exploring the website could be comprised of 4 different interactions.

  1. Pressed the on/off button
  2. Hovered over everything to see the locations of each button
  3. Interacted with the memory minigame
  4. Increased the difficulty of the memory minigame

The majority of my time was spent with the memory minigame and experiencing the optional increase in difficulty. This was due to the activity being the main focus of the website and having the option to increase the difficulty allowed me to spend more time on the game.

My most common action was pressing the symbols that were involved with the memory minigame. As the game offered 11 different interactable buttons, I easily spent the majority of my time pushing those buttons. As such, pushing those buttons was my most common action.

My impression of the web page's primary goal is that the creators wanted to hook people by presenting an engaging interface and allowing them to have fun within the confines of the website. From here, the users would be interested in seeing more work from the site creator via the social links provided at the top right area of the page. This was achieved by presenting a fun interactive minigame in addition to a fun talking robot that commented on the user's actions.

As the interface was engaging and kept me interested for a decent period, I would say that the goal was communicated well. The main draw of the minigame left me fixated on the page, wondering what more the website and its developers could offer. The use of pleasing colour combinations, chrome and orange, created a satisfying and enjoyable experience. Plus, the satisfying button pressing also contributed towards the feeling of satisaction.

The experience should be experienced multiple different times. This is due to the repeatable nature of the memory minigame and the different options to increase or decrease difficulty. Although, as this is the main draw of the site, a user should only spend around 5-10 mins on it. The experience could also include multiple different interactable games to showcase what the website developers are capable of and further interest a passing user.

The site encourages multiple repeat interactions through text that prompts the user to engage continuously with the memory minigame. The option the test a variety of difficulties encourages users to come back and experience different ways to play the website's interactable game. This is further exemplified throguh the use of bright orange text that is targed towards what the user is interacting with.

The experience references digital video through the use of the virtual camera, displaying a video game village. Video is also utilised to present text and a virtual face that incentivises the user to remain on the website by using text to communicate to the user. The website is reminiscent of early 2000's flash game websites that have fun and interactable games similar to Downstream.
